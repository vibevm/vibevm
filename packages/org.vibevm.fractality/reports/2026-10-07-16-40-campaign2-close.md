## What was done, end to end

One day, two sessions, seven phases. The campaign built the entire
initiative loop: boss sessions + run attribution in MC
(`FRACTALITY_BOSS_SESSION` via CLAUDE_ENV_FILE), the pure
`fractality-initiative` engine (scoreboard render, nudge policy,
route calculus — strictly factual by D7), seven new CLI verbs
(`session scoreboard route hook statusline harness fetch`), the CC
adapter under the availability law (any failure → silent exit 0),
threshold nudges with cooldown, stop-time parked-question pushes
with folded acks, profile auto-answer rules, the matrix as embedded
data with 10/10 goldens — and then measured the whole thing against
six cold GLM bosses in a pre-registered A/B trial.

## The decisions taken (the main thing)

1. **Honesty over the bet.** The falsification is recorded verbatim;
   no post-hoc threshold tuning, no metric surgery. The trial's
   pre-registration proved its worth the first time it was
   inconvenient.
2. **The levers become mandate seeds, not patches** — DEF-C2-1
   (a mid-work injection seam that exists in headless sessions),
   DEF-C2-2 (worker-credibility facts on the boss surface + a fixed
   staging toolchain), DEF-C2-3 (the cold-board problem), DEF-C2-4
   (re-run design: multi-prompt, N>3, Opus-class boss when budget
   allows).
3. **RP2 recommendation stands on data:** post-tool-use counter
   default-ON (P95 51 ms, half the budget). RP3 (settings target)
   keeps the `.claude/settings.local.json` recommendation. Both
   remain owner-open.
4. **The adapter is production-shaped:** across six hostile-cold
   runs, hooks installed/fired/exited-0 flawlessly, sessions
   attributed, counters counted, and no boss session was ever
   broken — the availability law is field-proven.
