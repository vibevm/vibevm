## Ideas and reflections

The falsification is more valuable than a confirmation would have
been, because the transcripts say precisely *why* B failed — three
mechanisms, all fixable:

1. **The nudge never had a chance to fire (F23).** UserPromptSubmit
   is the threshold channel, but a `-p` session has exactly one
   prompt, and it arrives at slate=0. The bosses crossed the
   threshold at 34/44/45 work-tools — all AFTER the last prompt.
   The teeth of the system were structurally disengaged by the trial
   modality. (Pre-registered as a caveat in MT-C2-04; the trial
   upgraded it from caveat to falsifier.)
2. **An empty scoreboard is anti-proof (F25).** Every arm-B boss was
   greeted with "0 runs · 0 completed all-time" — a fresh scratch
   home has no wins to show. The one channel that DID deliver
   delivered a reason to ignore the fabric.
3. **Worker credibility beat worker capability (F24).** The scratch
   `env -i` broke rustc's MSVC auto-detect; every boss hand-fixed
   its own linker, and two explicitly reasoned "delegated workers
   would hit the same broken cargo and cannot self-verify → keep".
   A staging defect handed every boss a *rational* keep argument —
   in both arms equally, so the A↔B delta survives, but absolute
   rates are depressed.

The bosses were not delegation-hostile: arm A runs 1–2 delegated
tasks 1/3 through the matrix exactly as designed (packets, workers,
review — the fabric worked end to end under a boss that had never
seen it). What the bosses did was *reason* — and our surface lost
the argument. GLM-5.2 quoted the matrix's own never-delegate
vocabulary back at us ("review-of-delegated-output is never-delegate",
"overhead exceeds the work"). A policy artifact written to encourage
delegation armed a fluent skeptic.

## The decisions taken

- The trial ran exactly as pre-registered: 3+3, frozen scoring, no
  peeking-then-tuning. The falsification is recorded as-is; no
  post-hoc threshold surgery.
- P3's falsifier analysis names the three levers above as
  DEF-C2-1/-2/-3 in the plan's §15 — they are the follow-up
  campaign's mandate seeds, not silent patches to this one.
- Run artifacts stay in `target/trial-results/` (uncommitted build
  state); the durable record is the MTs + this report.

## Left undone / unfixed

- Owner sign-off on the MT index (agent pre-ran and recorded; the
  pass is the owner's to record — manual-tests law).
- The staging toolchain defect (F24) — deliberately NOT fixed
  mid-trial (it would have unfrozen the protocol between arms);
  fix rides DEF-C2-2 before any re-run.
- The nudge channel in `-p` — a design gap, not a bug; needs the
  DEF-C2-1 study, not a hotfix.

## Global / strategic

The scoreboard-first posture survives — nothing blocked, no boss
session was ever harmed by the fabric (availability law held across
all six runs, hooks silent-exit included). But Campaign 2's core
bet — "visible wins + a well-timed nudge move cold propensity" —
now has a measured counterexample in the headless modality, and the
next campaign should treat *interactive* sessions (where statusline
and multi-prompt nudges exist) as the arena where that bet is
actually testable.
